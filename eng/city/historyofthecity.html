History of the City

↩ Back

Farroupilha is a city located in the Serra Gaúcha, in the state of Rio Grande do Sul, with a rich and significant history that dates back to the early European colonizations of the region. The municipality’s origins trace back to the 19th century when Italian immigrants began arriving in Brazil, seeking better living conditions and new opportunities.

Farroupilha was officially founded in 1934, initially known as "Nova Vicenza" in tribute to the Italian province of Vicenza, the homeland of many early settlers. The city’s economy was initially driven by agriculture, particularly grape cultivation, which would later make Farroupilha one of Brazil's leading wine production centers. To this day, the municipality maintains its winemaking tradition, attracting tourists eager to explore local wineries and savor the unique flavors of the region's wines.

The name "Farroupilha" was adopted as a tribute to the Farroupilha Revolution, an important revolutionary movement that took place in Rio Grande do Sul between 1835 and 1845. The city thus stands as a symbol of resistance and the fight for freedom, values that continue to resonate in local culture. Alongside its Italian heritage, Farroupilha has significant historical relevance within the gaúcho context, reflected in local festivities such as Semana Farroupilha (Farroupilha Week), which celebrates the state’s traditional culture.

Today, Farroupilha is a vibrant tourist destination, blending history, culture, and rich gastronomy. Visitors can explore traditional wineries, participate in local festivals like Fenakiwi and Fenachamp, and discover a city that, while it has grown and modernized, keeps alive the spirit and traditions of its Italian roots.
