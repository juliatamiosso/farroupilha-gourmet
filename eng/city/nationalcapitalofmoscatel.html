National Capital of Moscatel

↩ Back

Since 2019, Farroupilha proudly holds the title of National Capital of Moscatel, a recognition of its excellence in the production of this unique and enchanting wine. Thanks to its privileged climate and fertile soil in the Serra Gaúcha, the city has become one of Brazil's main wine-producing hubs, especially in cultivating the Moscato grape, which gives rise to the famous Moscatel sparkling wine.

With a winemaking tradition passed down through generations, Farroupilha’s producers have mastered the art of crafting light and aromatic wines with a hint of sweetness. Whether at large wineries or small family-owned estates, the passion for winemaking is undeniable, and Farroupilha’s sparkling wines are recognised for their excellence both in Brazil and internationally.

To celebrate this rich cultural and gastronomic heritage, Farroupilha hosts the Festival do Moscatel (Moscatel Festival), an annual event that brings together local producers, tourists, and wine lovers for an immersive experience in the city’s winemaking culture. During the festival, visitors have the chance to sample a wide variety of Moscatel wines and sparkling wines, paired with cuisine specially prepared to harmonize with the unique characteristics of this beverage. The festival also features themed dinners, live music, and a cultural program that highlights the Italian traditions that shaped the city.

Visiting Farroupilha is an opportunity to learn about the production process and, of course, to taste this refreshing and elegant wine that is part of the city’s identity. Here, each glass of Moscatel celebrates the history, labor, and dedication of the local winemakers.
